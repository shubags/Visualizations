Project Data Set Description and Motivation

The data...

SIPRI is the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute is the preeminent source for military spending by countries over time. The data source is frequently cited by governments and used by organizations to identify trends in the defense industry. SIPRI's site can be found here.

Permission to use the data for public consumption is typically required. However, there are a few exceptions, including the use of the data for use in a university/school/college paper, essay or dissertation. This includes producing graphs and charts based on the data. That being said, since I will be posting the results of the project online, I have requested permission nevertheless.

The data includes several perspective, but the most prevalent information for my needs is the military expenditures for:

  • NATO countries: data is available for the past 65 years
  • Non-NATO countries: data is available for the past 26 years

This numerical data is shown in terms of U.S. dollars, local currency, and as a percentage of GDP.

The motivation

I spent many years focused on analyzing the global defense industry. I would like to create a visualization of some of the data I did analysis on by never really visualized. I have linked the defense spending, by country, by year, but I intend to combine this with GDP growth data from the IMF. There are interesting trends about how certain countries have increased spending as a percentage of GDP while others have significantly decreased spending. I am also interested in the trends as they pertain to growth in manufacturing (data which is often available through the OECD).
