Data "Sketch"

Project Planning

Planned Tools

In addition to D3, I plan to use R to do my data preprocessing and analysis.

Because I plan to use an animated time series, I plan to use certain techniques in R to impute missing values. Depending on the complexity, I will simply impute the data using basic linear interpolation in R.

Planned Techniques

I plan to start with an animated bar chart of total defense spending per country. The x-value will be country, the y-value will be US Dollars spent on defense, and the color will be the continent on which the country comes from.

I will then build an area plot that shows a parallel coordinates plot that includes: the following axes:

  • Total defense spending during the entire period between the starting date and ending date
  • Total spending on social entitlement programs during the same period of time
  • Cumulative Average Growth Rate (CAGR) of spending on defense
  • Cumulative Average Growth Rate (CAGR) of spending on social programs

I plan on using an animated bubble plot, similar to the one that is used in the famous Health and Wealth visualization. For this plot, I will use four dimensions of data:

  • X-value: Debt as a percentage of GDP
  • Y-value: Defense spending as a percentage of GDP
  • Bubble radius: GDP of country
  • Bubble color: Continent that the country resides

Planned Interaction

The bar chart will allow users to select the year that they wish to see spending and the bars will re-build and re-order based on the year.

The paralell coordinates plot will allow the user to highlight the line of the country of interest so they can easily see the shape of the line of each country of interest.

The final visualization will be animated by year, and users will be allowed to see what the situation looked like during any given year by moving their mouse over the slider area.
